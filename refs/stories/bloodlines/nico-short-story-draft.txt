Back at it. I should probably get used to not seeing anything we work on pop up on the news, but every "quiet" federal request still lands the same way in my gut. I was tired from the last case, so on the drive I tried to steal sleep where I could, jacket rolled up against the window, because once we hit Groveton I knew that would be over. Strange thing is, getting away from the concrete actually helped - fields and tree lines feel more honest than polished offices and filtered briefings.

At the courthouse I stayed mostly in my lane and went straight to the medical records. Honestly, I would have preferred bodies in front of me, but photos and reports were what we had, so I combed through them anyway and caught myself wondering how normal people would react to spending an afternoon studying death this closely. Blunt-force trauma, fatal blood loss, no clear weapon profile - enough to confirm what happened, not enough to confirm how. When we locked in that these were dump sites and not kill sites, my first thought was simple: this rabbit hole is about to get deep.

Once we split tasks, I remember thinking this was going to be a long night and wishing I had gotten a full warm belly first, but daylight mattered more than comfort. At Kessler's site, the tracks and blood trail made me think, *don't love this*. Then we found fresh crossing sign and wheelbarrow tracks, and I could not shake one question: if this was this visible, why hadn't locals or the FBI followed it harder? By then my body was already doing the thing it does - deep breaths, deliberate steps, scanning ahead of my own thoughts.

By the time the Ethan-like lead converged with what we were seeing in the field and we reached the Whitlock farmhouse, all that background noise turned into one clear priority: do not go in blind. Seeing movement in that window and hearing those voices inside made it obvious something was off, but not obvious enough to justify shooting first. So I stayed where I could still make good decisions, held my breath for a beat, and tried to pull control back into our hands: "COME OUT AND PUT YOUR WEAPONS ON THE GROUND!"
